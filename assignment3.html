A3: Getting started with 3D printing

By Priyana Patel



Assembly and Setup

I assembled my printer slowly over three days following this helpful tutorial online (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ibsOYzXduYc). Surprisingly, I had no major issues apart from putting some parts on the wrong way and re-tightening some bolts. I adjusted the extruder's eccentric nuts at the end so it glided without wiggling. I also adjusted the build plate level and checked for loose screws.

Bed Leveling Protocol

My main challenge throughout the entire assignment came from the bed leveling protocol. Initially, I had adhesion issues because my extruder was too far from my build plate. After adjusting each corner, my extruder was too close to the build plate and scratched the surface. Each time I adjusted, the top left corner of my plate seemed to be too close to the extruder despite winding the knob all the way to the end. I then adjusted the eccentric nuts under the plate to re-level my plate. This did the trick, and I could finally run the print with my filament sticking to the plate. Unfortunately, I had to waste a lot of filament to complete this procedure.

Cylinders



Nested Object I took inspiration from the nested rainbow children's toy for my nested object. I created my small inner arch in Rhino and added a line at the bottom. I then created two additional arcs composed of two half circles joined with two lines at the bottom of each end. I then extruded the three objects to 3 cm in height, and each arc was 1 cm apart from the other. I scaled my structures to 500% (instead of 1000% like my other prints) to convert to mm in Cura to save time. I kept all of the default print settings the same and sliced under standard quality.
